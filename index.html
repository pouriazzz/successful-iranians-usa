Notable Iranian Americans

Maryam Mirzakhani

Mathematician, Fields Medal Winner

The first woman and first Iranian to be awarded the Fields Medal (2014), mathematics' most prestigious honor, for her outstanding contributions to the dynamics and geometry of Riemann surfaces.

Pierre Omidyar

Entrepreneur, Founder of eBay

Founded eBay in 1995, one of the world's largest online marketplaces. His innovative platform revolutionized e-commerce and created a new economy of online entrepreneurs.

Firouz Naderi

Former NASA Director

Led NASA's Mars exploration program and served as associate director at NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory. He oversaw successful Mars missions including Spirit, Opportunity, and Curiosity rovers.

Anousheh Ansari

Entrepreneur & Space Explorer

First female private space explorer and co-founder/CEO of Telecom Technologies. She is also the co-founder and chairwoman of Prodea Systems and sponsored the Ansari X Prize.

Quick Facts About Iranian Americans

Iranian Americans contribute over $280 million annually to charitable causes in the United States

There are more than 3,500 Iranian-American physicians in the U.S., many serving in underrepresented medical specialties and rural areas

Over 70% of eligible Iranian Americans participate in U.S. elections, higher than the national average

Iranian-American owned businesses employ more than 280,000 Americans across the country

During the COVID-19 pandemic, Iranian-American organizations raised millions for relief efforts and medical supplies

Iranian Americans have won numerous prestigious awards including the Fields Medal, Nobel Prize nominations, and MacArthur "Genius" Grants

Iranian Americans have founded science and technology education initiatives reaching thousands of American students

Several groundbreaking medical treatments and devices have been developed by Iranian-American researchers

Major Areas of Contribution

Science & Technology

Iranian Americans have made significant contributions to computer science, biotechnology, and engineering, with many holding leadership positions at major tech companies like Google, Apple, and Microsoft.

Medicine & Healthcare

Thousands of Iranian American physicians and medical researchers are advancing healthcare across the United States, from pioneering new treatments to providing care in underserved areas.

Education & Research

Iranian Americans serve as professors and researchers at top universities, contributing to American leadership in higher education and scientific research at institutions like Harvard, Stanford, and MIT.

Business & Entrepreneurship

From startups to Fortune 500 companies, Iranian Americans have created thousands of jobs and driven economic growth through innovative businesses and investments in various sectors of the economy.

About This Project

This website aims to highlight the significant contributions that Iranian Americans have made to the United States, showcasing their achievements in various fields including science, technology, medicine, business, arts, and culture.

Our goal is to provide accurate information about the Iranian-American community and demonstrate how cultural diversity strengthens America's social fabric and drives innovation and excellence.

We believe it is important to distinguish between the Iranian people and the Iranian government. Iranian Americans are among the most educated, law-abiding, and productive citizens in the United States. Travel bans and policies that restrict Iranian people from entering the U.S. separate families and prevent talented individuals from contributing to American society.

The Iranian-American community consists of individuals who have embraced American values while celebrating their heritage. They represent the best of both cultures and serve as a bridge between nations. Treating Iranian Americans and Iranians seeking to come to the United States separately from political issues with the Iranian regime is not only fair but beneficial to America's interests.

The data presented here is compiled from various reputable sources, including the U.S. Census Bureau, academic studies, community surveys, and publicly available information.
